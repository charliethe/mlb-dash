How This Works

This thing crunches numbers so you don't have to. Here's what's under the hood — no math degree required.

🔍 The Brain

Every game gets run through a model that weighs a bunch of stuff — who's hot, who's not, who's pitching, who's playing in a bandbox — and spits out a win probability for each team.

📊 What It Looks At

  • Winning percentage (half the weight) — mix of actual record and what "should" have happened based on runs
  • Run differential — are they blowing teams out or squeaking by?
  • Last 10 games — how've they been playing lately?
  • Pitcher matchup (MLB) — ERA, Ks, the whole deal
  • Ballpark — some stadiums are launching pads, some are pitcher paradises

💰 The Odds

  • When available, we grab real lines from sportsbooks via The Odds API
  • When not, the model calculates its own odds with a built-in 4% vig (the bookie's cut)
  • Edge = our win probability vs what the odds imply. If we think you've got a 60% chance but the odds say 50%, that's edge

🎯 Confidence

  • HIGH (75%+) — everything lines up, model is screaming at you
  • MED (60-74%) — decent signal, some noise
  • LOW (<60%) — ¯\_(ツ)_/¯ maybe skip this one

📈 Bankroll

  • You start with 100 units (fake money, real feelings)
  • Each pick risks 1 unit — boring but responsible
  • Pushes happen when the total exactly hits the line. You get your unit back and a firm handshake

⚾ Data

  • MLB — official stats API (real standings, pitchers, schedules)
  • NBA / NFL / NHL — odds only, no team stats (those APIs cost money, and we're not made of it)
  • Odds refresh every 5 minutes because the free tier is 500 requests/month and we're not trying to burn through it in a day

Built by someone who definitely should have been doing something else. Not financial advice. Actually, it's not even advice. It's a spreadsheet with feelings.
